I chose Computer Science as my major on a whim.


I had finally made a decision that was a long time coming, I was going to drop out of pharmacy school. I couldn't stand it, it was dull, none of the topics seem to have any underlying logic, and I knew enough about chemistry to know that the underlying logic wouldn't appear for a quite a while. I knew I liked games, and I knew that I really enjoyed figuring out how in-game physics worked. I thought that maybe I'd enjoy the logic required to make those things happen. With that small interest I moved home and enrolled as a Computer Science student. Luckily enough for me I actually enjoy programming.

I want to found, and successfully cash out of, a startup. I've got the right type of personality, I'm not partically exceptional at anything. Just pretty good at most things. I have a relatively high threshold for pain, and I get obsessive about solving a problem. Why would I want to walk that path though? I realized a long time ago that if you want to change anything signifiant, you've got to be able to pay for it. I acknowledge this is unrealistic and that I'll probably fail, but I've got to try.
